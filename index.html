Project

Electric Propulsion System for CubeSats

Click here to Download Project Report

Design and analysis of a Pulsed Plasma Thruster(PPT) integrated into a 3U CubeSat. CubeSat's lack a propulsive device, therefore their mission life is limited. We can extend the lifetime of CubeSats by using thrusters, which can also be employed to maintain the orbit and increase its orbit.

Modal analysis and Random vibrations testing were performed on the CubeSat frame and with the thrusters mounted inside CubeSat. There are a total of 8 thrusters that are used to control and also achieve three-axis control. PTFE(Teflon) is used as the propellant, two Copper strips are used which act as electrodes.

Orbital transfer using finite burn type maneuver

Finite Burn maneuver in STK

There were two sections: Apogee raise and Perigee raise. It took around six months to transition from 400km to a higher orbit with an apogee of 535km and a perigee of 515km. At 400km, the CubeSat's estimated lifespan was 1.2 years. Its expected lifespan rose to 4.7 years with the help of the thruster and the altitude it attained.

Power Analysis (Solar Panel groups)

Solar Panel tool in STK

Solar panel power profile at 20 degree inclination of CubeSat

The maximum power produced by all the solar panels was around 9.117 (W), the solar panels Minus X and Minus Y produced the most power compared to the other two panel groups. The maximum power produced by the Minus Y panel was around 6.513 (W), whereas the Minus X panel produced maximum power of around 6.512 (W). The power range was about 2.813 (W) – 2.787 (W).

Modal Analysis and Random Vibrations test

Modal analysis is used to determine the vibration characteristics of linear elastic structures.It is also the typical common dynamic simulation for many other dynamic simulations. Modal analysis looks for natural frequencies and normal modes of a structure. Natural frequencies and modes are some of the most important properties of a structure; they determine how the structure will respond.

Random vibrations test investigates the structural response to acoustic noise due to engine vibration and noise, air friction at the beginning of the launch phase. The instantaneous magnitude of the response is not constant over time due to several possible frequencies exert on the structure at the same time. Therefore, the problem should consider in a statistical and probability distribution approach before transforming to frequency domain. The Acceleration Spectral Density (ASD) also known as Power Spectral Density (PSD) is a statistical measure of the structure response to random dynamic loads. It is a graph of the PSD versus frequencies in which PSD can be displacement, velocity, acceleration, or force. In case of acceleration the unit can be presented in ((g2/Hz)/Hz).
